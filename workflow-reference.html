Payment Arrangement Workflow

How to use the tenant payment arrangement form for your property management company.

The Workflow at a Glance

  1. You complete the Pre-Fill Tool. Open the manager pre-fill tool. Enter the tenant name, property, lease info, and outstanding balance.
  2. Generate the unique link. The tool builds a one-time link with the tenant's information already locked in. Copy the link or use the built-in email template.
  3. Send the link to the tenant. Email or text the link. The tenant opens it and sees their information pre-filled and locked.
  4. Tenant completes only what's needed. Tenant proposes payment dates, amounts, optional hardship explanation, and signs electronically.
  5. Submission lands in your inbox. You receive a clean, formatted email with the full proposal. Review and respond with your written approval, modifications, or denial.
  6. Send the final agreement for signature. If approved, send the formal signed agreement to the tenant. The arrangement is in effect once both parties sign.

Who Fills In What

You (Property Manager)

  • Tenant name(s)
  • Property address
  • Lease start and end dates
  • Monthly rent amount
  • Total outstanding balance
  • What the balance includes
  • (Optional) Tenant email and phone

The Tenant

  • Confirms or corrects email and phone
  • Proposes payment dates and amounts
  • Selects preferred payment method
  • Explains hardship circumstances (optional)
  • Confirms or denies prior arrangements
  • Reads and acknowledges all terms
  • Types their full legal name as signature

What the Tenant Sees

When the tenant opens the link, the form will show three different field types. Make sure your tenant understands what they mean.

Gold border = Locked Set by the property manager. Cannot be changed by the tenant. (Property, balance, lease dates, etc.)
Green border = Pre-filled, editable Suggested by the manager but tenant can correct typos. (Email, phone.)
Plain field = Tenant fills in Empty fields the tenant completes themselves. (Payment dates, amounts, signature.)

Critical Reminders

This is a proposal, not an agreement. A submitted form does NOT create a binding payment arrangement. The arrangement only takes effect when YOU provide written approval. Until then, the tenant remains fully obligated under the existing lease.
Continue rent collection as normal. The tenant's regular monthly rent obligation is unchanged. Arrangement payments are IN ADDITION to rent — not a replacement. Continue posting late fees and pursuing collections per your normal practice until the arrangement is formally approved.
You are not required to approve. Payment arrangements are a courtesy, not a tenant right. You may approve, deny, modify, or counter-propose. You may also choose to file for eviction instead, even after receiving a proposal.

Frequently Asked Questions

What if the tenant tries to change the locked information?
They can't. The locked fields are protected at the form level and cannot be edited or submitted with different values.
What if the tenant claims the locked information is wrong?
Generate a new link with the corrected information. The original link still works, but you should generate fresh links for each round of negotiation to maintain a clean audit trail.
Can I see what the tenant's link looks like before sending it?
Yes. Use the "Open to Preview" button in the pre-fill tool. It opens the tenant's view in a new tab so you can review before sending.
What if the tenant never submits the form?
Nothing happens. There is no obligation created by sending the link. Continue your normal collections process, including filing for eviction if appropriate.
Where do submitted proposals go?
To the email address you set in the pre-fill tool's setup section. You can also CC a second address (assistant, paralegal) for redundancy.
Can I generate a link for a tenant whose email I don't have?
Yes. The tenant email is optional in the pre-fill tool. Generate the link, then send it via text message, paper letter, or any other channel.
What if I need to change something after I send the link?
Generate a new link with the corrected information and ask the tenant to use the new link instead. Don't try to edit the original — just send a fresh one.
Is the submission legally binding on the tenant?
The proposal is signed by the tenant and acknowledges all terms, but until both parties have a fully executed agreement (proposal + your written approval), it is not enforceable. Treat it as a settlement offer subject to your acceptance.

What to Do When You Receive a Proposal

  1. Verify the balance. Cross-reference the tenant's submitted balance with your ledger. They should match.
  2. Evaluate the schedule. Are the payment amounts realistic? Will the balance be paid off within a reasonable time (typically 30–90 days)?
  3. Consider the hardship explanation. Does the explanation make sense? Has this tenant had prior arrangements? Is current rent being paid on time?
  4. Make a decision. Approve as-is, propose modifications, or deny. Communicate in writing.
  5. If approving — execute the formal agreement. The proposal is a starting point. The actual binding agreement should be a formal document signed by both parties.
  6. If denying or filing eviction — document your reasoning. Save the proposal, your response, and the decision rationale in the tenant file.

Important: This form and workflow are for use under South Carolina law. Consult your attorney for matters in other jurisdictions.
